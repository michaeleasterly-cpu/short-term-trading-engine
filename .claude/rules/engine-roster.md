# Engine roster SoT (ECR-only)

Canonical SoT: `tpcore/engine_profile.py::_PROFILE` (frozen pydantic-v2 dict; the single mechanically-enforced source for what engines exist / order / cadence / lifecycle). Heavy-lane rule applies (see `heavy-lane`).
Authoritative external: <https://code.claude.com/docs/en/extend>, <https://code.claude.com/docs/en/memory>.

Hard rule:

- **`_PROFILE` membership is edited via the ECR ONLY** — `docs/superpowers/checklists/engine_change_request.md` is the structured touchpoint; `python -m ops.engine_sdlc --ecr <file>` is the canonical CLI; the operator approves ADD/REMOVE via binary `APPROVE? (y/n)` on a planner-validated diff. MODIFY (re-tuned params past DSR≥0.95 ∧ cred≥60) + LAB→PAPER promote are automated, no approval.
- **Never hand-edit `_PROFILE` or any of its derived shadows.** The 22-site-drift lesson (PR #170, Sigma archive) is the why.
- **Non-Python shadows are sentinel-fenced regions** regenerated by `python scripts/gen_engine_manifest.py`. `gen_engine_manifest.py --check` reds CI on drift. Shadows: smoke loop, `run_all_engines.sh` / `platform_pipeline.py` docstrings, pyproject testpaths/include. Do NOT hand-edit inside a fence.
- **`tpcore/tests/test_engine_lifecycle_consistency.py`** is the N-way clockwork that fails the build on any half-state (e.g. an engine declared in `_PROFILE` without the LAB→PAPER capital-gate evidence; or a smoke-loop entry without `_PROFILE`).
- **`tpcore.engine_profile.lab_targetable_engines()`** (SP-B) is the Lab roster authority; the frozen anchor in `tpcore/tests/test_lab_targeting_consistency.py` + `test_lab_targetable_accessor.py` is updated only when the ECR adds/removes an engine.

Lifecycle states (SP1→SP4): `LAB → PAPER → LIVE → RETIRED`. `LIVE` is reserved (paper-only mandate). Sentinels (`sigma` RETIRED, `lab` LAB) keep the non-PAPER states real and exercised.

Half-retirement fails CI (per the symmetric `test_provider_lifecycle_consistency.py` for the data-feed roster).
