§ 04 / 07

Where the federal money actually goes

$812.8M in federal contract obligations flowed into the 5-county LWA over the last 24 months. The concentration on a single recipient is the natural consequence of how the channel works — ammunition contracts are large-dollar-per-job and one Marion-based facility happens to be the work locale. It's not that the local economy depends on one company; it's that the federal-contracting channel runs primarily through one operator.

What it means for the workforce board
When a single recipient captures the majority of federal dollars in a region but employs only a fraction of local labor, the board has standing to negotiate hiring commitments, apprenticeship slots, and local supplier-development — Community Benefit Agreement precedent. The same prime is a natural anchor for registered apprenticeships in machinist, electrician, industrial maintenance and quality tech ladders. Tier-2 supplier development is the underused lever: large primes use out-of-region subs; HUBZone-certified local firms (Franklin / Perry / parts of Jackson qualify) can absorb that work.

The Marion VA Veterans First contracting story. 4 of the top recipients are confirmed SDVOSBs capturing $17.6M (2.2% of regional total). Marion VA's Veterans First Contracting Program is the single biggest non-DoD federal procurement channel in the region — and the highest-value SBA certification to pursue for any local firm. The asymmetry: only 1 of 4 are local. The other three are headquartered in FL / KY / NC. The set-aside money flows; it just flows to out-of-region veteran firms because the region doesn't have enough certified local SDVOSBs. Smith Hafeli is the proof of concept — $11.9M in 24 months. There's no reason 5–10 more local SDVOSBs couldn't exist with the right certification on-ramp.

Travel-required, family-supporting opportunities

Several family-supporting credentials require travel — and the local training infrastructure exists to feed them. These pay more than any non-degreed local-employment path: $90k–180k+ all-in. Rotational schedules (14-on / 14-off offshore wind, IBEW project rotations, RR pool service) keep significant home time. CDL OTR is called out separately as family-time conflict — it's chronic absence, not rotational.

Anchor-employer attraction · data-center scorecard

Without new anchors paying above the livable-wage threshold, the training-alignment problem can't be solved by training alone. The realistic target list isn't Google or Microsoft; it's tier-2 firms hunting stranded power, federal agencies with relocation precedent, and university research-anchored programs. The data-center scorecard for LWA-25:

Stranded coal-plant interconnectBaldwin retirement · ~1,200MW MISO-South
Ameren IL serves the area. Hyperscalers and AI-training operators value stranded-grid sites for fast interconnect timelines.
